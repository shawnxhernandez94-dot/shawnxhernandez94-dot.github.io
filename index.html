About

Philosophy as a way of seeing clearly.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in philosophy at the University of Pennsylvania, where my research engages questions of justice, obligation, and moral standing — particularly as they surface in political life and social institutions.

Alongside my research, I work as a lecturer, designing curriculum that brings philosophical rigor into conversation with the real dilemmas students face. I believe careful thinking is a skill anyone can develop — and that it matters.

My work draws on analytic ethics, political philosophy, and continental thought, and is animated by a commitment to ideas that are not merely true but useful.
